I created Study Planner in 2012 as my first experience with software development and coding in general. It was a project primarily for myself and as a learning experience. However it really took off, even though I have left it unattended since 2014, it currently stands at over 170,000 downloads for Windows and Windows Phone.

When Windows Phone came out back in 2010, its unique take on a mobile UI inspired me to download the developer tools so that I could play around with the emulator. With no formal CS education in my school, looking at sample applications was quite intimidating. However during my GCSE exam period I was looking for a method of procrastination and I settled on a 20 part web programming course for .NET developers. This lead me to learn programming for the first time

After my exams, equipped with this new skill I started searching for a project to work on. Throughout my time in secondary school, I always redesigned the timetables we were given for our planners and I knew the struggles of remembering about homework. So I decided to create an app to replace the paper planners we used in school.
